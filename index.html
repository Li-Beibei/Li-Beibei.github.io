Beibei Li is currently an associate professor at the College of Cybersecurity, Sichuan University, P.R. China. He received his B.E. degree (awarded Outstanding Graduate) in communication engineering from Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, China, in 2014 and his Ph.D. degree (awarded Full Research Scholarship) from Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, in 2019. He was invited as a short-term visiting researcher at the Canadian Institute for Cybersecurity, University of New Brunswick, Canada and also the research group of NEtworked Sensing and Control (NESC), Zhejiang University, P.R. China. He is now serving as a Guest Associate Editor for the Frontiers in Blockchain and a Publicity Chair for the 2021 IEEE International Conference on Advanced and Trusted Computing (IEEE ATC).

Research Interests

Dr. Li's research interests span several areas in security and privacy issues on Internet of Things (IoT) and Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS), with a focus on:
  • IoT security
  • Big data security & privacy
  • Secure cloud computing
  • Artificial intelligence/Artificial immune
  • Cyber-physical security
  • Applied cryptography
  • Industrial control system security/Smart grid security
