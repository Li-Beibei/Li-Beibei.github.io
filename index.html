Beibei Li is currently an Associate Professor (Doctoral Advisor) with the School of Cyber Science and Engineering, P.R. China. He received his B.E. degree (awarded Outstanding Graduate) in communication engineering from Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, China, in July 2014 and his Ph.D. degree (awarded Full Research Scholarship) from Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, in Match 2019. He was invited as a short-term visiting researcher at the Canadian Institute for Cybersecurity, University of New Brunswick, Canada and also the research group of NEtworked Sensing and Control (NESC), Zhejiang University, P.R. China. He is now serving as a Guest Editor for the Special Issue "Security and Privacy for Machine Learning Applications" of Sensors and the Special Issue "Security and Privacy of the Internet of Things for Industrial Applications" of Sensors, as well as a TPC member for both the 2023 IEEE GLOBECOM'23 Main Symposium on Communication and Information System Security (CISS) , and the 2023 IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications(INFOCOM) and the 2023 IEEE 11th International Conference on Information, Communication and Networks (ICICN).

Research Interests

Dr. Li's research interests span several areas in security and privacy issues on Internet of Things (IoT) and Cyber-Physical Systems (CPSs), with a focus on:
  • IoT security
  • Big data security & privacy
  • Artificial intelligence/Artificial immune
  • Cyber-physical security
  • Applied cryptography
  • Industrial control system security/Smart grid security
